About

Stark’s work sits at the intersection of quantum computation, neural systems, and autonomous intelligence security. His research explores how quantum technologies and advanced AI architectures can be jointly leveraged to build high-assurance, adaptive, and resilient computational systems. He has previously developed quantum-hybrid cryptographic frameworks designed to secure AI-driven and autonomous infrastructures against emergent threat models.



Alongside his technical work, Stark maintains a strong interest in brain–machine interfaces, the dynamics of cognition under stress, and the broader implications of autonomy on societal governance. Outside the lab, he is an avid guitarist, writer, and martial artist — pursuits that continually shape his creative reasoning and scientific intuition.
